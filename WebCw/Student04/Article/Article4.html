FREDDIE MERCURY QUEEN BAND

Personal Life

Freddie Mercury was a famous singer, songwriter, and music composer in the 70's & the 80's. He was born Farrokh Bulsara on September 5, 1946, in Zanzibar, Tanzania. Mercury was born with four extra teeth in the back of his mouth, causing his now-famous bucktooth grin. In fact, his nickname growing up was Bucky. Mercury was open about his bisexuality, but he kept his relationships private. He spent time in a boarding school in Mumbai, India, where he studied Piano. After that Freddie moved to London with his family and he attended Ealing College of Art and he befriended with a lot of musicians around this time including Queen bandmates, drummer Roger Taylor and guitarist Brian May.

Mercury played with a few other bands before starting the Queen's Band. In 1969, Mercury joined a group called Ibex as their lead singer. Afterward, Freddie Started Queen's with Taylor, May &. bassist John Deacon in 1971, and they played their first gig together that June.

In 1973 they released their first self-titled album. Sheer Heart Attack (1974), featured their first hit, "Killer Queen," a song about a high-class call girl as their third record. That comes no.02 on the U.K. charts.

Mercury died of AIDS-related bronchial pneumonia on November 24, 1991, at the age of 45. In 2001, Mercury and the rest of the band received special recognition for their contributions to American music history when they were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

The movie Bohemian Rhapsody was released in 2018 which was based on the life story of Freddie Mercury. The movie starred Mr. Robot's Rami Malek as Freddie Mercury, follows the rise of Queen leading up to their legendary Live Aid performance in 1985.

BOB MARLEY

Personal Life

Robert Nesta Marley the whole world know him as 'Bob Marley'. Bob Marley was born on 6 February 1945 in Nine Mile, Saint Ann Parish, British Jamaica. The whole world renoun him as 'The King of POP'. The jamaican was a singer and a songwriter. Bob Marley's parents were Norval Sinclair Marley and Cedella Booker. Marley's father died of a heart attack in 1955 at the age of 70. After the death of Marley's father his mother married Edward Booker. With the marriage Marley had two step-brothers Richard and Anthony. Marley went to 'Stepney Primary and Junior High School' for his studies. On 10 February 1966 Bob Marley married Alpharita Constantia "Rita" Anderson in Kingston, Jamaica. Bob Marley had 11 children. Sharon, Cedella, David "Ziggy, Stephen, Robert "Robbie", Rohan, Karen, Stephanie, Julian, Ky-Mani, Damian were the children of Bob Marley. Bob Marley was a member of the Rastafari movement.

Musical Career

Bob Marley had a musical background because his parents were also musician so he was destined to be a musician too. When he was atteding at his High school he started playing music with his friend Neville Livingston. In 1962 Marley released his first single's "Judge Not". With it Marley began his musical career. And after he relased 3 more songs, "One Cup of Coffee", "Do You Still Love Me?" and "Terror". In 1963, Bob Marley with Bunny Wailer, Peter Tosh, Junior Braithwaite, Beverley Kelso, and Cherry Smith created the reggae group "The Wailers". The group did many songs together untill Braithwaite, Kelso, and Smith left the Wailers. The remaining Marley, Peter Tosh and Bunny Wailer remained as the Wailers and did what they always do that is creating music.

What's different in Marley's work is he wrote song that is only unique to him and also the rhythm, the beat which he uesed for his songs were unique only for him. The Jamaican culture is different than any other that it feel likes freedom, that's what Marley had in his songs with the culture of the Caribbean to express the freedom. Marley always believed that every one should be equal and everyone should have the freedom that they deserved, he wrote his songs with those intention to express the felling that he had towards the world.

1965 The Wailers relased their first album "The Wailing Wailers". That mark the begining of an new era of music. The time progressed the group released more albums like "Soul Rebels", "Soul Revolution", "The Best of The Wailers", "Catch a Fire", "Burnin". The group were popular not only in the Caribbean but also all around the world. After the break up of the Wailers, Marley continued to work in the group as "Bob Marley & The Wailers". In 1974 Bob Marley & The Wailers released their first album "Natty Dread". The album got popular with the song "No Woman, No Cry" and "Get Up Stand Up", which made a hugh impact on his music career.

In 1976 Marley left Jamaica to live in England. After he shifted to England he again started writing songs, which includes "Exodus", "Waiting in Vain", "Jamming", and "One Love". In 1978 Marley return to Jamaica to perform a show after he relased a album "Babylon by Bus" which was highly acclaim. 1980 Marley released his final studio album "Uprising" which include songs like "Redemption Song" and "Forever Loving Jah". The "Uprising" album was one of his most religious works that he did in his life.

Illness and death

At the age of 36 Bob Marley died on 11 May 1981 at Cedars of Lebanon Hospital in Miami. In 1977 the doctors found that Marley was diseased with a cancer, a type of 'malignant melanoma' under the nail of a toe. The doctors adviced him that his toe will be cut off, but Marley turened down the treatments because it would have efected his career. Despites his illness Marley didn't stop doing what he does. Marley's last live performence was on 23 September 1980, Stanley Theater in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Despites getting treatment for his illness it couldn't kept him alive for more. Althrough his life Marley inspired many people by his songs and with some great quotes, his final words were "Money can't buy life." Marley had a state funeral on 21st of May 1981. He was buried in a chapel near his birthplace with his guitar.

All around the world the people gave tribute to the legend in various way. The words that Marley said have touched hearts of many people, that's why even after his deth his legacy continued to inspire many more. Awards Honours

  1. In 1976 Bob Marley & The Wailers won the Rolling Stone Band of the Year
  2. In June 1978 Bob Marley was awarded the "Peace Medal of the Third World" from the United Nations
  3. In February 1981 Marley was awarded the "Jamaican Order of Merit"
  4. In March 1994 Bob Marley was Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame
  5. In 1999 Exodus album was declared the "Album of the Century" by Time Magazine
  6. In February 2001 Bob Marley was inducted to the "A star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame"
  7. In February 2001 Marley was awarded "Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award"
  8. In 2004 Rolling Stone ranked him No. 11 on their list of the 100 Greatest Artists of All Time
  9. In 2004 marley became the first inductee the "UK Music Hall of Fame"
  10. In 2010Marley's "Catch a Fire" inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame (Reggae Album)
